Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 6, 2026

Diurn is a private diary. We collect as little as possible, and we never read, analyze, or sell what you write.

1. What we collect

  • Your email address — used only to sign you in with a one-time code and to identify your account.
  • Your diary content — the entries, star ratings, and photos you seal. These are stored so you can read them back on any device.

We do not require or collect your name, and we do not run advertising or third-party tracking.

2. What we never do

We do not read, analyze, mine, sell, rent, or share your diary entries or photos. Your content is never used for advertising or to train any models. It exists only to be shown back to you.

3. Drafts stay on your device

While you are still writing, your unsealed draft — including any photos you have added but not yet sealed — is stored only in your own browser (local storage). It is not sent to our servers until you seal the entry.

4. How your content is stored

Sealed entries and photos are stored with our hosting provider (Supabase). Access is restricted to your account using row-level security, so other users cannot read your data. Photos are kept in private storage and shown to you through temporary, expiring links. Photos are compressed in your browser before upload.

5. Cookies & local storage

We use only what is necessary to keep you signed in and to hold your drafts and preferences on your device. We do not use advertising or analytics cookies.

6. Data retention

Your entries remain until you delete your account. Note that sealed entries are permanent by design and cannot be individually edited or deleted while your account exists.

7. Your rights

You may request access to, or deletion of, your account and its data at any time by contacting us. Deleting your account removes your entries and photos from our storage.

8. Security

We use reasonable measures to protect your data, but no online service can be completely secure. Please keep access to your email account safe, since it is the key to your diary.

9. Children

Diurn is not intended for children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

See also our Terms of Use.
